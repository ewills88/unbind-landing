Clio vs. Unbind: Best Practice Management for Divorce Attorneys (2026)

A detailed comparison of general-purpose practice management vs. divorce-specific tools.

If you practice family law, there is a good chance you have used Clio. With over 150,000 legal professionals on its platform, Clio is the undisputed market leader in legal practice management—and for good reason. It is polished, reliable, and backed by years of iteration. But market leadership in all of legal practice management does not automatically mean it is the best fit for every practice area.

Divorce and family law present a unique combination of challenges: high-emotion client communication, voluminous financial discovery, state-specific form requirements, and settlement negotiations that can drag on for months. General-purpose tools handle some of these well. They handle others with workarounds. And for a few, they offer nothing at all.

In this comparison we will look honestly at Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, and Unbind—where each excels, where each falls short, and which is the right choice depending on how your firm is built.

Where Clio Excels

Let us give credit where it is due. Clio has earned its position at the top of legal tech for several reasons that matter.

Integration ecosystem. With over 200 integrations—from QuickBooks to Lawmatics to Zapier—Clio connects to virtually any tool in your stack. If your firm relies on a particular calendaring app, intake CRM, or accounting platform, the odds are high that Clio already has a connector for it. No other practice management platform comes close to this breadth.

Track record and stability. Clio has been operating since 2008. It has survived multiple market cycles, processed millions of matters, and earned the trust of bar associations and legal aid organizations alike. When you choose Clio, you are choosing a product that will almost certainly still be here in ten years.

Multi-practice versatility. If your firm handles divorce alongside personal injury, estate planning, and immigration, Clio's generalist architecture is genuinely an advantage. You get one system, one login, and one set of workflows for everything. For firms where family law is one practice area among several, this simplicity is hard to beat.

Where General-Purpose Tools Fall Short for Divorce

The same generalist architecture that makes Clio flexible also means it was not designed around the specific pain points of divorce practice. Here is where that gap shows up in daily work.

Document categorization is entirely manual. In a contested divorce, a single case can generate hundreds of documents: bank statements, tax returns, pay stubs, appraisals, retirement account statements, credit card records. In Clio, MyCase, and PracticePanther, every one of these must be manually tagged and filed into the correct folder. Unbind uses AI-powered document categorization trained specifically on family law documents, automatically sorting uploads into the right categories and flagging inconsistencies.

Zero settlement negotiation tools. Settlement is the heart of most divorce cases. Attorneys spend hours building asset and debt spreadsheets, modeling different division scenarios, and going back and forth with opposing counsel. General-purpose platforms have no concept of a marital balance sheet, equitable distribution modeling, or proposal-counterproposal tracking. You end up in Excel. Unbind provides structured settlement workspaces where both attorneys can propose terms, compare scenarios side by side, and track negotiation history in one place.

Discovery management requires workarounds. Family law discovery has its own rhythm: interrogatories, requests for production, subpoenas to financial institutions, and the constant chase of incomplete responses. In Clio, you track all of this through generic tasks and calendar entries. There is no built-in concept of a discovery request, a response deadline, or a deficiency letter. Unbind models the full discovery lifecycle, from initial requests through responses and follow-up, with automated deadline tracking tied to your jurisdiction's rules.

Client communication lacks case-specific context. Divorce clients have questions that fall into predictable categories: "What happens to the house?" "How is custody decided?" "When is my next hearing?" General-purpose client portals show a timeline of messages and documents but do not organize information around the structure of a divorce case. Unbind's client portal gives each client a dashboard tailored to their case stage, with clear visibility into upcoming deadlines, pending tasks, and the current status of settlement negotiations.

Pricing: The Real Comparison

Sticker prices can be misleading. Clio's base plan starts at $49 per month per user, which is genuinely affordable. But the base plan does not include the features that most divorce attorneys actually need day to day.

  • Clio Essentials ($49/mo): Basic matter management, time tracking, and billing. No client portal, no document automation, no custom fields.
  • Clio Complete ($89/mo): Adds the client portal, task management, and some automation features. This is the tier most family law firms land on.
  • Clio Complete + Grow ($139/mo): Adds intake and CRM. If you are handling your own client acquisition, this is often necessary.

By the time a divorce attorney has the Clio features they actually use, they are paying $89 to $139 per month per user—and still lack settlement tools, support calculators, AI document categorization, and state-specific forms.

Unbind is $89 per month per user during beta, with every feature included. No tier gating, no add-on charges for the client portal, and no separate subscription for intake. The comparison is not $49 vs. $89. It is $89–$139 for general-purpose tools plus spreadsheet workarounds vs. $89 for a platform built from the ground up for your practice area.

Who Should Use What

Choose Clio if:

  • Your firm handles multiple practice areas and needs one system for everything
  • You rely heavily on specific third-party integrations that only Clio supports
  • You have existing workflows built around Clio and the switching cost is not justified
  • Family law is a small percentage of your overall caseload

Choose Unbind if:

  • Your firm specializes in divorce and family law
  • You are spending significant time on document organization, settlement modeling, or discovery tracking
  • You want a client portal that reflects the structure of a divorce case, not a generic timeline
  • You want AI tools trained on family law documents rather than general legal documents
  • You are tired of stitching together spreadsheets, shared drives, and workarounds to manage what should be core features
